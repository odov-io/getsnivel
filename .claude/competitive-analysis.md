## Executive Summary

Snivel is entering the scheduling/booking market with a fundamentally different approach:

1. **No free tier** - 30-day trial, then pay (avoids stale accounts, proves commitment)
2. **No feature gating** - All features available to all paying customers
3. **Pack-based pricing** - Not per-seat nickel-and-diming
4. **Org-first architecture** - Built for teams, simplified for solos

This positions Snivel as the **value leader** at exactly **half the price of Calendly** while offering comparable or better features for SMB use cases.

## Business Model Analysis

### Calendly
**Strategy:** Land with free, expand with features, upsell to enterprise

- Free tier is a lead gen tool (very limited functionality)
- Standard unlocks "expected" features that should be baseline
- Teams adds collaboration features
- Enterprise for security/compliance

**Weakness:** Per-seat pricing scales poorly for customers. A 50-person team pays $9,600/year for features Snivel includes at $2,100.

### Cal.com
**Strategy:** Open-source credibility, generous free tier, monetize teams

- Free tier is genuinely usable (attracts developers/startups)
- Revenue comes from team features and enterprise compliance
- Open-source builds trust but fragments the market

**Weakness:** High per-seat cost at scale. Organizations tier at $37/seat is expensive. 50 users = $22,200/year.

### CalendarBridge
**Strategy:** Niche positioning (calendar sync + scheduling), simple pricing

- No free tier (aligns with Snivel philosophy)
- Pricing based on calendar count, not features
- Lower price point than Calendly/Cal.com

**Weakness:** Less known, feature set more limited, hybrid focus dilutes scheduling message.

### Snivel
**Strategy:** Value leader, no feature games, org-first

- No free tier (30-day trial proves commitment)
- All features unlocked for all paying customers
- Pack-based pricing rewards growth
- Org-first architecture simplifies team scaling
- Consistent 16.7% annual discount across all tiers

**Strengths:**
- Exactly ~50% of Calendly pricing at every tier ("half the price")
- No feature anxiety ("do I need to upgrade for X?")
- Clean upgrade path (solo → team packs)
- Domain intelligence for organic growth
- $3/user floor at 100+ users (still 81% cheaper than Calendly)

**Risks:**
- Less brand recognition (new entrant)
- Missing some integrations (roadmap items)

## Key Insights

### 1. The Free Tier Trap
Calendly and Cal.com use free tiers to acquire users, but this creates:
- Stale accounts (the Scribe problem)
- Support burden for non-paying users
- Feature-gating complexity

**Snivel's "no free tier" is a feature, not a limitation.** It attracts serious users and avoids the abandoned account problem.

### 2. Per-Seat Pricing Punishes Growth
At 50 users:
- Calendly Teams: $9,600/year
- Cal.com Teams: $9,000/year
- Snivel Team 50: $2,100/year (78% cheaper)

Customers FEEL this. Pack-based pricing makes Snivel the obvious choice for growing teams.

### 3. Feature Gating Creates Friction
Competitors gate basic features (reminders, integrations) behind paid tiers. This creates:
- Constant "should I upgrade?" anxiety
- Sales friction during evaluation
- Resentment when you hit a wall

**Snivel's "all features included" eliminates this entirely.**

### 4. Enterprise ≠ SMB
Calendly and Cal.com are building for enterprise (SSO, SCIM, SOC2, Salesforce). This means:
- Complexity SMBs don't need
- Pricing SMBs can't afford
- Features SMBs won't use

**Snivel can own the SMB market by NOT building enterprise complexity.**

### 5. Magic Link > SSO
Enterprise buyers demand SSO. SMBs don't care - they want simple.
Magic link authentication:
- No passwords to manage
- No SSO integration needed
- Actually MORE secure for most use cases

**This is a feature, not a missing capability.**
